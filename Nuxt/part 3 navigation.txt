<h1 style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 18pt;"><strong>Computer | Laptop Port</strong></span></h1>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">For communicating any two-piece of hardware you may require some interface to connect them. In a computer system, we called this connector as the port. So, in general we can say computer/laptop port is nothing but just an interface or point of connection between peripheral devices(input/output devices) to the computer. Technically they are basically slots on your motherboard.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Computer Port is also called a communication port because they are responsible for communication between the computer and peripheral devices.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-size: 18pt;">The function of Computer Port:</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">The main purpose of this port is allowing one hardware component to be plugged in another so that they can communicate with each other.</span></p>
<h3><span style="font-size: 18pt;">Computer|Laptop Port Shape and Size</span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Port comes in a wide variety of shapes such as round(PS/2), square( RJ 45), rectangular (FireWire, USB, etc), trapezoidal(DB-25 printer port, DB-9 serial port). But in general this shape doesn't differ from one vendor to another. Most of the vendors follow the same common standardization for making computers and their port.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">In a laptop or computer, ports can be divided into two groups depending upon how port allows the communications. These two groups of ports are serial ports and parallel ports.</span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Now, let's describe each of them:</span></p>
<h3 style="text-align: justify;"><span style="text-decoration: underline; font-size: 18pt;">Serial Port</span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Serial Port is an interface through where serial hardware devices communicate with the computer by transmitting<strong> one bit of information at a time</strong> over a single communication line. </span></p>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">&nbsp; &nbsp; Example: Used for older computer mouse (before ps/2 port and USB port are invented), older keyboard, external modems, and PDAs.It is less commonly used in today's tech industry.</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: left;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="font-size: 18pt;">Parallel Port</span></span></h3>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Parallel Port is an interface through where parallel hardware devices communicate with the computer by transmitting <strong>multiple bits of information at a time over multiple communication lines</strong> (or wires).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Example: Used for scanners and printers, external CD ROM, Zip Drive, external HDD, tape backup devices, etc.</span></p>

<h3 style="text-align: left;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><span style="font-size: 18pt;">3) PS/2 Port</span></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">PS/2 port stands for Personal System/2 port which was introduced to the world by IBM in 1987. It was used by older PC for connecting mouse or keyboard to an IBM PC compatible computer system. It is round and consists of 6 pins. In most cases, green color PS/2 port is used by mice and purple color PS2/ is used for keyboard.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Some drawback of PS/2 port system are :</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Hotplugging</strong>: It is said that it is the bad practice of removing mouse and keyboard cable from ps/2 port while the system is running. It may damage the system.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;"><strong>Durability</strong>: PS/2 connectors are not intended to turn on and off regularly, which may bring about twisted or broken pins if not inserted in the proper orientation.</span></li>
</ul>

But even we can find this port in an older PC.</fieldset>
<h3 style="text-align: left;"><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong><span style="font-size: 18pt;">4)USB Port</span></strong></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">USB stands for Universal Serial Bus. The USB port can transfer data at much faster rate than serial and parallel port.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">There are various types of USB port presence in today's laptop and desktop market. Some of them are :</span></p>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type A</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type B</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type C</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type A Mini</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type B Mini</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type A Micro</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type B Micro</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type B Micro USB3</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">Type-A USB Port</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 14pt;">It is a common type of USB port present in general personal laptops and desktop. It comes with 4 pins connector and is available in various types and versions. They are USB 1.1, USB 2, USB 3.0 (USB 3.1 Gen 1), and USB 3.1 Gen 2. Although all those have 4 pins but we can identify each of them by color.</span></p>

<h3><span style="font-size: 18pt;">Type B USB Port</span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">They are not common as Type-A connectors. Type B connector is almost square in shape. It is extensively used while connecting printer cable, scanner, USB hub, and external hard drive cable to the computer.</span></p>

<h3><span style="font-size: 18pt;"><strong>Type C USB Port&nbsp;</strong></span></h3>
<p style="text-align: justify;"><span style="font-size: 14pt;">This is the future of USB port and is expected to replace both Type A and Type B port and many laptops and mobile companies are taking it as a standard USB port. The data transfer rate is <strong>10 Gbps</strong> to <strong>20 Gbps</strong>. It is much slimmer and smaller with 24 pins which makes it capable of handling <strong>high current</strong> and <strong>speeds</strong>. Every USB Type C port is a reversible plug which means they can be plugged upside down if you want. Some of its types are: <strong>USB 3.1 Gen 2</strong> and <strong>USB 3.2</strong></span></p>
