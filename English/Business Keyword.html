Costs:

Money that is going out of the business.

Startup Cost

Cost required for business to start up

Operating Cost

On-going cost that business has on day to day basis.

Fixed Cost

A cost which doesnot change with output(use).

Variable Cost

A cost which does change with output(use).

Total Cost = Fixed + Variable Cost.

Direct Cost

A cost that is directly linked to the production of service or product.

InDirect Cost

A cost that is not directly linked to the production of service or product.

Revenue ( राजस्व )

Income generate by selling goods and services to the customer. 

i.e All money that is going into  (or coming into ) the business.

Revenue =  Qty sold * price per unit

Profit

Profit = Total Revenue - Expenditure.

Profit occurs when total revenue is greater then expenditure.

Expenditure

Money that business pays out.

Overheads

Every day running cost of buiness

 

Break Even

Point where there is no profit or loss. ie. Revenue = Expenditure cases.

Net Monthly

Difference between predicted inflow and outflow for each month.

Opening Balance 

The figure that has been carried forward from previous month's closing balance.

Closing Balance 

The figure that is in the organizations bank account at the end of current month.

Inflation ( मेहङगाही  ==== मुद्रास्फीति )

English: raising in the prices of services and goods in a particular country, resulting in a fall in the value of money.

Nepali : Inflation means बस्तु को मुल्य बद्नु र पैसा को मुल्य घट्नु.

Example:  It takes Rs. 10 to buy wai in 2005 and Rs. 20 in 2015. So inflation is high ie. prices is rising. 

Note: When inflation rises then each dollar will buy fewer goods than before.

Inflation Rate:

Change in prices in 1 year. 

If the inflation rate is 3% then your salary must grow more than  3% of current salary to cope with inflation.

Eg. Salary: Rs 25000 

then

  • 3% of 25000 = Rs 750

So, your growth must be more than Rs 750 each year to cope with inflation.

 

Note : Inflation rate of Nepal : 6.1% in 2020 AD
