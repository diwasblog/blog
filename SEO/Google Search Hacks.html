Idea Description Example

Search for a specific site

 Put "site:" in front of a site or domain to get all content of the site.

site:youtube.com

site:ourtechroom.com

Search for related sites

Put "related:" in front of a web address, you already know. 

Will give sites related to entered sites

related:quora.com

  • Gives sites like yahoo answers, StackOverflow, medium

related:youtube.com

  • Gives sites video sites like youtube.

 

See Google’s cached version of a site

Put "cache:" in front of the site address.

The query cache:url will display Google’s cached version of a web page, instead of the current version of the page.

 

On the cached version of a page, Google will highlight terms in your query that appear after the cache: search operator.

cache:ourtechroom.com

Search for an exact match

Put a word or phrase inside quotes. 

"tallest building".

Exclude words from your search

Put - in front of a word you want to leave out. 

jaguar speed -car

Search within a range of numbers

Put .. between two numbers. 

 camera $50..$100.

Search hashtags

Put # in front of a word. 

#throwbackthursday

Search for a price

Put $ in front of a number.

camera $400.

Search social media

Put @ in front of a word to search social media. 

@twitter.

 

 

 

 

Search in Page Title

Title page tag is an HTML tag for a web page, it defines what page is about. If you are dealing with a coffee machine and want to know more about your competitors in a similar domain, you can search in the page title and see what kind of product do they have.

Eg. 

allintitle:"format computer tricks"

Will display the article having exactly in the same order on the title of the webpage.

format intitle:"computer help"

Must have computer help in search title and format keyword anywhere on title.
