Share: Ownership of people in the company. Percent of Ownership.

Stock: The collection of shares of a single company.

Sharemarket: Platform for buying/selling shares. In the share market, the public limited companies are listed.

ShareExchange: A place in which different investments are traded. The most well-known exchanges in the United States are the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE). In Nepal, we have NEPSE (Nepal Stock Exchange)

Suppose any company like Infrastructure Development Limited wants to open share in public then they have to take permission from the Security Board of Nepal( SEBON )· नेपाल धितोपत्र बोर्ड. 



If a company wants a large amount of money for any developmental activities, there are two main option

1) Loan :

  • The company can collect the only a certain amount in the loan.
  • Has to pay interest for loan.

2) Share Market (Best one)

  • Collect huge Money from public by giving ownership.
  • No need to pay interest
  • Share money among ownership.

Security Bond / Government Securities: When the government wants to develop any project then they create government securities and government securities raise capital via sharemarket.

What is Nepse?

Nepal Stock Exchange Limited (abbreviated as NEPSE) is the only Stock Exchange of Nepal.It has almost 300 registered companies registered in Nepse.

Nepse gives full information of current stock exchange information of all 300 companies. If green then good, if red then bad. Based on which nepse increase/decrease is known.

Types of Market in Share Market

Primary Market 

  • For starting any business, IPO (Initial Public Offering) is launched for the first time by that business company to offer share to the public.
  • In Primary Market you can only buy a share, but cannot sell a share.

Secondary Market

  • If you want to sell the share, then you have to go to the Secondary Market.
  • Here, trading occurs(buying and selling between peoples)

DEMAT Account:

  • Account to store share.Which is to open by individuals to do stuff in the share market.
  • When in 7 days of creating DEMAT Account, you will get DEMAT No.

C-ASBA :

  • After filling C-ASBA form, you will get CRN no. This number is needed while filling IPO.
  • Banks and financial institutions give CRN (C-ASBA Registration Number) to each investor after verifying the beneficiary account of the investor. Only investors who have obtained the CRN number can apply for the purchase of shares from their bank account.

Mero Share :

  • Should be filled out mero share form for using MEROSHARE APP to fill out IPO.
  • Whatever transaction you have done in share can be seen on MeroShare APP.

Where to get IPO is Opened Information ?

After login , in MeroShare app, under C-ASBA in menu, you will get currently opened IPO.
