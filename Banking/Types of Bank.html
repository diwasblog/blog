Central Bank(केन्द्रीय बैंक)

  • Nepal Rastriya Bank is the Central Bank of Nepal
  • It provides guidance and regulating the banking system of the country
  • It is government banks
  • It is known as a bankers bank

Commercial Bank

  • It accept deposits and grant short-term loans to its customer.
  • It gives long-term and medium-term loans to business enterprises.
  • They also provide housing loans on a long-term basis to individuals.

Public Sector Commercial Bank ie. Where the majority of stake is held by the government of Nepal.Eg. Banijya bank,Nepal bank

Private Sector Commercial Bank ie. where the majority of stake is held by private individuals.Eg. Machhapuchre bank, Siddharta bank

Foreign Bank ie. They are registered and have their headquarters in a foreign country but have their branches in our country. Eg. Bangladesh Bank

Cooperative Banks (सहकारी Bank)

Cooperative societies are formed by people who come together and form a society jointly so as to serve their common interest.These societies are formed under the cooperative societies Act.When a cooperative society engages in banking businessit is called cooperative bank. Eg. National Cooperative Bank Limited,Nepal Multipurpose Coopeartive Society(NMC)

Development Bank (विकास बैंक )

Development Banks are financial structures that have a primary agenda of aiding infrastructural Development and providing loans to the agricultural and industrial sector.

Eg. Kabeli Bikas Bank, Gandaki Development Bank

The primary source of funds for Commercial Banks is the money deposited by people, while a development bank gathers it’s funded by borrowing from the government or selling securities.

Microfinance( लघुवित्त => लघु => Small,  वित्त => Amount ) 

Microfinance is a banking service provided to unemployed or low-income individuals or groups who otherwise would have no other access to financial services.

Actually, what happens in the rural area generally people are unemployed and has low income and their reach to banks and other financial sectors for getting loans are not possible. So, various microfinance is established in rural areas and they provide loans for basket-making, sewing, street vending, and raising poultry activities, etc to the farmers and unemploy people and finally raise the income of the rural people.

The intention of Microfinance is to reduce poverty (गरीबी) of rural people.

Microfinance provides

  • Micro Loan
  • Micro Saving 
  • Micro Insurance

Microfinance is targeting सानो किसान .

Microfinance cos are trying to meet a genuine need of small businessmen who are unable to have access to organised financial markets like banks or not able to sustain higher interest charged by local money lenders. If you say 12 percent is charged by banks and 36 percent per annum and above is charged by local money lenders, microfinance charge around 24 percent or so.

Microfinance has helped many people rise above poverty and transform their lives for the better. I've known countless households who have used microloans to permanently increase their cash incomes, which are then used for longer and better education for the families' children as well as improving health and nutrition. I believe this type of successful outcome is the most common result of a microfinance loan.

Microfinance loans can also do harm, when businesses fail to earn enough profit to cover the cost of interest. Microfinance institutions worldwide typically charge interest rates of 30% to 40% in order to cover the costs of managing small loans in the traditional, staff-intensive way. 

Why are microcredit interest rates higher than traditional interest rates?

Higher operating costs 

On a day-to-day basis, microcredit agents are out in the field, sometimes in villages that are difficult to access, meeting lenders and following up on loan repayments. It is a time consuming job that is at the core of the microcredit business. So, has to pay to its staff.

Commercial vs Investment vs Investor Banks

Commercial Banks offer services to the general public, but there are some banks that offer services to the companies and investors but not to the public. They are investment and merchant banks.

Merchant (व्यापारी) Bank Investment Bank
It is a bank that undertakes international finance and underwriting of securities. It is a bank that acts as an intermediary between the client and investing public by helping them in raising fund.
It deals with large companies It deals with small companies
Eg.  Nepal SBI Merchant Banking Limited Eg. Nabil Investment Banking Limited

Issuer Bank vs Acquire Bank

Issuing Banks are the banks in charge of issuing credit cards and debit cards to consumers.This is any bank that provides cards to its customers.

Acquiring Banks are banks that work with merchants and merchant services companies to provide merchant accounts and collect the money owed to merchants from issuing banks. 




What is Merchant?

  • A merchant is a company or individual who sells a service or goods.
  • A merchant can be a wholesaler or a retailer, and the products can be sold from any one source to any other source.

Different Type of Merchant:

1) ECommerce Merchant: An eCommerce merchant or an online merchant is someone who sells products or services exclusively over the Internet. 

2) WholeSales Merchant: A wholesale merchant or wholesaler will typically purchase goods in bulk and redistribute the products to retailers in smaller quantities.Usually, a wholesale merchant would operate out of a large establishment such as a warehouse in order to store stock before distributing it to retailers and individuals.

3) Retail Merchant: A retail merchant or retailer buys merchandise from wholesalers and sells them to end-users or consumers, usually in small quantities. 

What is an Agent?

The Agent may be the person or persons that connected the merchant to a processing partner, managed the application, and set up a process to get the Merchant approved and enabled to process.

Agent/Broker vs Employee

Employees work for a particular company and get a fixed salary, but an agent is like a medium between two different people and acts as a representative of a particular field or company in commercial aspects.
