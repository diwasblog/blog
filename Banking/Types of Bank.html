Types of Bank

  1. Central Bank
  2. Commercial Bank (Private & Public)
  3. Cooperative Bank 
  4. Development Bank
  5. Institutionalized Banks(Trust and Life Insurance)

Central Bank(केन्द्रीय बैंक)

  • Nepal Rastriya Bank is the Central Bank of Nepal
  • It provides guidance and regulating banking system of the country
  • It is government banks
  • It is known as bankers bank

Commercial Bank

  • They accepts deposits and grant short-term loans to its customer.
  • It gives long-term and medium-term loans to business enterprises.
  • They also provide housing loans on a long term basis to individuals.

 Public Sector Commercial Bank ie. Where the majority of stake is held by the government of Nepal.Eg. Banijya bank,nepal bank

Private Sector Commercial Bank ie. where the majority of stake is held by private individuals.Eg. Machhapuchre bank, Siddharta bank

Foreign Bank ie. They are registered and have their headquarters in a foreign country but have their branches in our country. Eg. Bangladesh Bank

Cooperative Banks (सहकारी Bank)

Cooperative societies are formed by people who come together and form a society jointly so as to serve their common interest.These societies are formed under the cooperative societies Act.When a cooperative society engages in banking businessit is called cooperative bank. Eg. National Cooperative Bank Limited,Nepal Multipurpose Coopeartive Society(NMC)

Development Bank (विकास बैंक )

Development Banks are financial structures that have a primary agenda of aiding infrastructural Development and providing loans to the agricultural and industrial sector.

Eg. Kabeli Bikas Bank, Gandaki Development Bank

The primary source of funds for Commercial Banks is the money deposited by people, while a development bank gathers it’s funded by borrowing from the government or selling securities.
