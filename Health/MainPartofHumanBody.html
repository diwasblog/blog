Heart

  • The heart is a little tilted towards the right.
  • The heart acts as a pump to force blood into all our body parts.
  • As blood flows, it carries with it the nutrients and oxygen to all body parts.

Kidney:

  • The job of the kidneys is to remove waste and extra fluid from the blood.
  • The kidneys take urea out of the blood and combine it with water and other substances to make urine.

Lungs

  • Lungs help us to Breathe.
  • As we breathe in, oxygen from the surroundings flows into our lungs
  • As we breathe out, Carbon Dioxide(a waste product) flows out of the lungs.
  • Left Lungs are smaller than Right Lungs.

Liver

The liver's main job is to filter the blood coming from the digestive tract, before passing it to the rest of the body.


liver is the only internal organ that can regrow
Liver helps in
  • Bile production and excretion.
  • Excretion of bilirubin, cholesterol, hormones, and drugs.
  • Metabolism of fats, proteins, and carbohydrates.
  • Enzyme activation.
  • Storage of glycogen, vitamins, and minerals.
  • Synthesis of plasma proteins, such as albumin, and clotting factors.

Liver Diseases Types:

Hepatitis: Inflamation of Liver.Usually caused by viruses like hepatitis A, B, and C.
