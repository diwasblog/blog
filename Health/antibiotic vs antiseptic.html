Antiseptic

An antiseptic is a substance that stops or slows down the growth of microorganisms.

They’re frequently used in hospitals to reduce the risk of infection during surgery and other procedures.

Example: Beterdine, dettol, sprint

Antiseptic is used for washing hands, Cleaning skin before an operation,Treating skin infections.

 

Antibiotic

Antibiotics are responsible for killing bacteria as well as parasites.

A parasite is a type of germ that needs to live on or in another living being (host).

Antibiotics do not work against infections that are caused by viruses (for example, the common cold or flu), or fungi (for example, thrush in the mouth or vagina), or fungal infections of the skin.

Example: Penicillins  , Setamol 

Immune system and Antibody

Immune System

The main function of the immune system is to prevent or limit infection.

Vaccines

Suspension of some inactive part of microorganisms (like polio, Hepatitis, etc) into our body so that our body produces antibody(protein ie. fighting agent)  and if the same microorganisms attack our body in near future then antibody of that microorganism which is already present will automatically kill that organism.

We inject polio vaccines in small ages, then our body develops antibodies for poliovirus and finally kills that . Then our body learns how to kills poliovirus. And when the same poliovirus attacks us in the future then the antibody of polio which was already present in the body will automatically kill that virus.
